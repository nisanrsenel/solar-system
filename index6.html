PLANETS

MERCURY


Mercury—the smallest planet in our solar system and nearest to the Sun—is only slightly larger than Earth's Moon. Its surface is covered in tens of thousands of impact craters. Despite its proximity to the Sun, Mercury is not the hottest planet in our solar system. But Mercury is the fastest planet, zipping around the Sun every 88 Earth days.

VENUS


Although Venus is not the closest planet to the Sun, it is the hottest planet. This is because it has an atmosphere containing intense greenhouse gases. Venus is a very active planet, with mountains and volcanoes on its surface. The dimensions of Venus are very close to the Earth. An important feature that distinguishes Venus from other planets is that it rotates around itself in the opposite direction of the Earth and many other planets. Therefore, the sun rises from the west, sets from the east.

EARTH


The Earth has a solid crust, a surface that includes active volcanoes, mountains, valleys, canyons, plains and many other landforms. Earth is special because it is an ocean planet. About 70% of its surface is made up of oceans and seas. Its atmosphere consists mostly of nitrogen and contains a lot of oxygen for us to breathe. In addition, its atmosphere protects us from meteorites, meteors entering our atmosphere disintegrate and then reach the earth's surface.

MARS


Mars is a cold desert planet that is half the size of Earth. It looks red because of the iron in its soil. For this reason, it is also called the ”Red Planet". Like Earth, Mars has seasons, poles, volcanoes, canyons and weather events. The atmosphere is very thin. It consists of carbon dioxide, nitrogen and argon. Currently, water exists on Mars only in sparse clouds and icy soil. A day on Mars lasts 24.6 hours.

JUPITER


Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System. Its atmosphere is covered with swirling cloud streaks. In the place called the ”Great Red Spot", there are storms that have been going on for hundreds of years. Jupiter is a gas giant, so it does not have a solid surface. It is thought that there is an Earth-sized solid core at its center. Jupiter also has rings, but they are too pale to be seen by the eye.

SATURN


Saturn is a gas giant just like Jupiter and consists mostly of hydrogen and helium. It has an intense atmosphere. Saturn is famous because of its rings. This ringed planet has 7 rings with gaps between them. These rings are made up of pieces of ice and rock.

URANUS


Uranus consists of water, methane and ammonia liquids above its small rocky center. Its atmosphere is composed of hydrogen and helium, as on Jupiter and Saturn. It also contains methane. The substance that gives Uranus its blue color is methane. Uranus has also pale rings. Uranus, an ice giant, is the only planet that rotates sideways. And it also turns from east to west.

NEPTUNE


Neptune, the last planet in our Solar System, is dark, cold and very windy. Neptune, an ice giant like Uranus, consists of dense water vapor, ammonia and methane gas on top of a solid center that is the same size as the Earth. Its atmosphere consists of hydrogen, helium and methane. Just like Uranus, it is methane that gives Neptune its blue color. Neptune has 6 rings, but they are very difficult to see.
